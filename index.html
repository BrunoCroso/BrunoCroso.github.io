Academic Research

Text-Enriched Graph Neural Networks for Detecting Disinformation Campaigns on Social Media - PUB Scholarship

University of São Paulo

I was involved in an academic research under the guidance of Professor Roseli de Deus Lopes, investigating the incorporation of textual features into Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for fake news detection on the social network X (formerly Twitter). Our research examined how integrating representations of user profile content and interactions involved in the spread of news into the graph's nodes influenced model performance. Additionally, we explored the impact of different embedders for generating these representations, comparing the widely-used static text encoder GloVe with the contextual encoder BERTweet. Finally, we also investigated whether upsampling combined with feature noise injection could enhance results, applying the NEFTune method with noise amplitudes of 0, 5, 10, 15, 20, and 25. All possible feature combinations were trained using the Graph Attention Network (GAT) architecture and the results were evaluated using the Wilcoxon signed-rank test for paired differences.

The extended abstract developed was accepted and presented as a poster at the Learning on Graphs Conference (LoG Conference) (2024) and is available at openreview.net.

Jan 2023 - Jan 2025

The relationship between the body composition of women in early pregnancy and fetal biometry and adiposity in the third trimester

University of São Paulo

For my second final project in my Bachelor's Degree, I collaborated as part of a duo responsible for the statistical analysis of the research project in question. This analysis sought to assess the influence of variables relating to the body composition of pregnant women in early pregnancy, such as the amount of fat mass in different parts of the body and BMI, on variables relating to fetal biometry and adiposity in the third trimester of pregnancy, such as fetal weight and cranial circumference. The study consisted of two main parts:

  • Analysis of the explanatory variables individually: We fitted linear regression models to assess the individual impact of each variable relating to the body composition of pregnant women in early pregnancy on the variables of the fetuses.
  • Analysis of the set of explanatory variables: We applied Principal Component Analysis and linear regression models to assess the overall influence of the explanatory variables on the response variables.
Aug 2024 - Dec 2024

Analysis of spatial and temporal patterns of infant mortality before and during the COVID-19 pandemic in Pernambuco

University of São Paulo

For my first final project in my Bachelor's Degree, I collaborated as part of a duo responsible for the statistical analysis of the research project in question. This analysis aimed to investigate changes in infant mortality in the State of Pernambuco by comparing the pre-pandemic and post-pandemic periods. The study consisted of three main parts:

  • Profile Analysis of Epidemiological Variables: We applied chi-square and proportion tests to determine if, and how, the pandemic affected the profile of infant mortality.
  • Temporal Analysis: Time series techniques were used to identify trends in variables associated with infant mortality between 2017 and 2022.
  • Spatial Analysis: Local and global Moran’s I tests were employed to detect spatial patterns and assess clusters of municipalities with similar behavior during both the pre- and post-pandemic periods.
